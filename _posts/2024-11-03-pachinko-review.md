## One line review - Pachinko

Min Jin Lee writes four generations of a Korean family in Japan with remarkable patience. No character is a symbol, everyone is just a person trying to get through. The way discrimination shapes lives across generations without ever becoming the explicit point of the book is impressive. Dense but never slow.
